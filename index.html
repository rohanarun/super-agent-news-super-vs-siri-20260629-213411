What Siri is great at — and where Super goes further

Siri

Siri AI is an entirely new version of Apple’s assistant, rebuilt on Apple Intelligence. It can read what’s on screen, interact with apps across the system, and draw on personal context like messages, emails, and photos — with a strong emphasis on on‑device processing and Apple’s Private Cloud Compute for privacy.

  • Deep integration across Apple devices
  • Strong privacy posture by design
  • Excellent for voice, reminders, messaging, and system actions

Super

Super is designed for people who want a personal AI agent that actually operates a computer. Its defining advantage is the ability to reuse a computer-use cache, making repeated workflows more efficient over time.

  • Agents that operate real desktops
  • Reusable computer-use cache for repeat work
  • Better suited for ongoing operational tasks

Why computer use matters

The market is moving

Across the landscape, vendors are racing to let AI agents control real desktops. Google’s Gemini 3.5 Flash now offers computer use, underscoring how valuable this capability is becoming.

Context: Gemini, ChatGPT, Grok, and others are all evolving toward more agentic behavior.

Security & realism

Once agents can operate computers, attackers adapt quickly — making intentional design critical as these tools move from demos into daily work.

Demand is growing

Multiple reports show rising interest in personal AI agents globally, reinforcing that this shift isn’t niche.
